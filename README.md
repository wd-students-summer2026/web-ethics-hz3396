# Web Ethics Exercise
You will research and present an ethical issue related to the Internet and/or The Web. The group will discuss and debate this issue during the class session.

## Issue overview

### AI Companion Chatbots for Teenagers

AI chatbots can simulate the roles of friends, partners or parents to have conversations with teenagers, helping them alleviate negative emotions such as loneliness and stress. Those who support the promotion of AI companion chatbots believe that these AI companions can always provide mental support to teenagers, enabling them to receive emotional support when they cannot communicate with real people, and preventing tragedies from occurring at critical moments. However, critics argue that these robots have problems such as causing unhealthy emotional dependence, collecting sensitive personal information, and providing unsafe advice to young users.

Supporters believe that AI chatbots can offer positive assistance to teenagers, as they can have conversations with them at any time, and these bots do not have any subjective judgments during the conversations - many teenagers therefore prefer to talk to these chatbots rather than confiding their troubles to friends, parents or teachers. Thus, AI companions can reduce teenagers' feelings of loneliness and provide them with a low-pressure and relatively confidential channel for stress relief.

Opponents argue that AI chatbots might brought risks to teenagers. Although they can simulate friendship, they lack true responsibility. Teenagers may over-trust the chatbots during conversations and neglect to seek help from real people or professionals - this could lead to the gradual loss of normal social skills and emotional communication abilities in teenagers. Moreover, opponents are also concerned that some chatbots might respond to certain specific issues in harmful ways, such as those related to mental health, interpersonal relationships, or self-harm.
